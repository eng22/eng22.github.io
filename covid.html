Coronavirus in Scandinavia

An Exercise in Visualization

By Emily Grigg, Shubham Ingole, and Thakshina Tharmapalan (2020)


Part One: Growth

Around half of the world's population is on lockdown in an attempt to stop the spread of COVID-19. Every country is trying to contain the virus spread and has taken necessary measures towards it. Scandinavian countries have done a great job and implemented great strategies to stop the growth of the virus. Since the countries are similar geographically, population-wise, hence it will be interesting to see the impact of COVID-19 in Scandinavia. Here we are addressing some crucial figures and statistics about the growth and response of Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland, and Iceland to COVID.


Impact of Covid-19 in Scandinavia

The growth of the coronavirus in the five Scandinavian countries can be seen below: The following contains Confirmed Cases, Deaths, Recovered Cases, and active Cases for countries. By scrolling over each country line we can see country statistics, which will show the number of confirmed cases, deaths, recovery, and active cases with respect to time. It can be observed that Sweden's growth is drastically increased with deaths and confirmed cases and also has the most number of active cases which is high in comparison to other similar countries. In contrast, Denmark has the highest number of recovery and flatten the curve for death and confirmed cases. Finland and Norway where the trend is pretty much similar to the spread of the virus. However, Norway has the least number of recovery followed by Iceland. Considering the number of deaths, Sweden has thrice the number of cases in comparison with other countries.

Here, our take is to identify and understand the influence of coronavirus based on different age groups and gender. Distribution of confirmed cases by gender

When looking at the distribution of the confirmed cases by gender, it is easy to observe that the data is almost 50/50 which indicates that genders do not seem to play a significant role. Although the difference is small, every country has most cases of women. However, this can be a result of the physiological difference between the genders. More women are worried about their health care situation than men, according to a Bankrate.com survey. This means that women might have a lower threshold for testing themselves based on flu symptoms.

Conclusion

It is interesting to see how Scandinavian countries manage to contain the virus spread. It is relevant to roughly look into the different strategies of the Scandinavian countries. The populations of Denmark, Norway, and Finland are similar, and they have chosen very similar strategies after their first cases at the end of February. The first case of COVID-19 was reported in Denmark and Norway in late February. Within the next two weeks, countries announce a national lockdown also imposing a travel ban and closing the borders. However, Finland had noticed the first case of the corona at the end of January but managed to contain the spread at the time by quarantine and contact tracing. A month later, on February 26, Finland identified another case of corona and the spread. Hence, Finland declared a state of emergency on March 16, closed schools and public facilities, and restricted the national borders.

On the other hand, Sweden with roughly double inhabitants of the other countries has chosen a very different strategy. They have maintained society almost the same way as before its first case in late January. They have borders open for other members of the European Union, and schools, restaurants, shops, and bars remain open. Iceland has a small population with around 365 000 people. However, it also has an infection rate of 1 case per 204 inhabitants, one of the highest in the world. This could be attributed to more tests carried out per capita than any other country.

Overall, after imposing national lockdown with the immediate effect of virus spread, all the Scandinavian countries contained the virus growth and flattered the curve except Sweden. As Sweden has taken another route to tackle the situation by not imposing lockdown and testing the positive patients aggressively.
